Janvi Shah
google, apm
Janvi Shah (’15) is an Associate Product Manager at Google working on Google Search features for media and entertainment. At Duke, she majored in Neuroscience with minors in CS and Finance, founded StartupConnect, and spent her summers at DukeEngage in Nicaragua, Duke in Silicon Valley/Geneva and an internship at Rock Health (a digital health startup seed fund). As a product manager, she strives to combine her background in consumer psychology and technology, working with engineers and designers to build products that add value to users’ lives at scale. Yusuf Simonson
the muse, cto
Yusuf writes software that keeps themuse.com running smoothly to provide a seamless experience for Millennials in search of their dream job and accessible career advice. Previously, Yusuf worked as a developer at companies large and small, including Google, Docker, and Microsoft. A Forbes contributer and member of its Tech Council, Yusuf is a North Carolina State alum and received his masters in Computer Science from UNC at Chapel Hill. Melissa Dalis
square, data scientist
Melissa is a data scientist at Square in San Francisco. A 2014 graduate in Computer Science and Math, she will be talking about building machine learning systems at scale, fraud detection and data analysis at Square, and her path to data science. Chris Morton
blockscore, founder
Chris Morton is an engineer at heart. He studied electrical engineering, graduated at the peak of the internet boom and headed to Silicon Valley. He first went to IBM, but quickly took the leap to startups right before the dot com crash. Fortunately the startup he joined, Andiamo, was acquired for nearly $1 billion. Chris decided to shake up life and moved to Durham to do his MBA in 2007. Once there, he sold real estate and worked on his own company during those two years. The SF bay area called him back after Duke and he started Cube, a company he took through Y Combinator in 2012. He got pushed out of that company, started working in Bitcoin, and joined two Stanford CS sophomores to found BlockScore, another company he took through Y Combinator in 2014.
